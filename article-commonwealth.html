Commonwealth of People

Why real estate needs a different kind of order; a practical frame for governance where choices become decision-grade capital signals.

7 min read Hobbes Commonwealth Cost of Carbon Governance

Real estate runs on promises across many hands—owners, lenders, tenants, operations teams, ESCOs, insurers. Each optimizes locally, so risks that matter most (energy volatility, overheating, carbon liability, reputational blow-ups) live in the gaps. Tools help, but they mostly sit at the edges of the problem.

  • Market data tells you relative rent/price, not how comfort & energy choices expose you to ecological or policy shocks.
  • ESG reporters lift disclosures to investor-grade, but rarely turn meter data into enforceable operating commitments with payback, verification, and recourse.
  • Analytics/BMS finds anomalies, yet “fix the spike” isn’t the same as governing against a public ecological floor with ROI in capital terms.

The result: fragmentation and blame-shifting. In equities you can diversify idiosyncratic risk away. In property you can’t—assets are few, large, and unique. That’s why we propose a Commonwealth of People.

What “commonwealth” means

A covenant to keep order over things no single firm controls—carbon cycle, grids, breathable air—via a public, published, non-optional floor that sits above private contracts. States keep sovereignty; professional and civic bodies supply evidence and critique.

Instruments for practice

1) Commonwealth Cost of Carbon (CCC)

A public rate everyone can reference in underwriting, budgeting, and leases so retrofit ROI doesn’t hinge on fuel-price luck.

2) A shared ecological ledger

A governed record of assured promises—baseline, method, factors, versions—so claims are inspectable, not just asserted.

3) Alarms & rulings

Breaches fire alarms with shared priority; rulings define what good looks like and how success is verified (acceptance & M&V).

4) Executive function

Rulings are peer-reviewed, explained plainly, commissioned in situ; disputes can escalate; everything is explainable.

Why now

Rates ↑, energy noisy, policy tighter, tenant expectations higher. A Commonwealth of People supplies the shared floor, the public carbon rate, fair enforcement, and the ledger to prove it.
