Alarm → Action → Plan → M&V

The operating rhythm that turns data into enforceable decisions—with proof.

Governance Operations Evidence 6 min read

The loop at a glance

  1. Alarm. A shared breach (e.g., P2 electricity overrun; overheating hours) with context and priority.
  2. Action. A proposed fix (bundle of measures) with capex, savings, NPV, β exposure change, comfort impact.
  3. Plan. Ownership, dates, dependencies, and acceptance criteria.
  4. M&V. How success will be verified (method, window, factors) and where evidence is stored.

What’s inside each step

Alarm

  • Standard priority ladder (P1 critical → P4 optimisation).
  • Evidence: time window, baseline and factors used for the check.
  • Link to a ruling (playbook) if one exists.

Action

  • Bundle: e.g., LED + schedules, ASHP + controls, shading + NV tuning.
  • Economics: NPV/IRR, payback, confidence, scenario band.
  • Impact: FEP delta, β shift, tCO₂e, comfort/overheating badge.

Plan

  • Owner, start/finish, dependencies (permits, tenant windows).
  • Acceptance criteria (e.g., ≥15% vs modelled baseline; Δ comfort ≤ +0.02 risk).

M&V (measurement & verification)

  • Method: Bills 12m (weather-normalised), or sub-meter before/after 90d, etc.
  • Factors & versions: emissions factors, degree-day sets.
  • Where evidence lives: Lineage entry with baseline, method, factors, license and version history.

Example (office, gas-heavy)

Alarm: P2 Electricity Overrun (12-month window).
Ruling R-E.12 applies → measures: schedule optimisation + LED.
Plan: owner Ops; start 01-Oct; acceptance ≥15% reduction vs modelled baseline; M&V by bills (12m), weather-normalised.
Evidence: ledger entry links to baseline, method, factors, versions.

Why this beats ad-hoc projects

  • Portable responsibility. Everyone understands the rule, the action, and the test.
  • Fairness & legitimacy. Playbooks are peer-reviewed; disputes follow a clear procedure.
  • IC-ready. Each action carries a decision memo (economics + evidence plan) by default.

Use-cases

Budget overrun

P2 alarm proposes a ranked list of fixes; you choose the bundle with the best NPV band and acceptable comfort impact.

Overheating in summer

NV/mixed-mode ruling suggests shading + control tweaks; acceptance is a target reduction in warm-hours with logger evidence.

Green-capex approval

Attach the plan & M&V to the IC paper; funding is tied to a testable outcome and a ledger entry.

Quick start

  1. Adopt the shared priority ladder (P1–P4) and a small rulings library.
  2. Require acceptance criteria + M&V on every action.
  3. Store evidence in Lineage—baseline, method, factors, versions.
  4. Use Hoshi’s Actions to package alarm → action → plan → proof.
