Hoshi in 5 minutes

From utility bills to decision-grade signals (NPV, β, systematic vs idiosyncratic).

5 min read Investors Operators Signals

TL;DR

Hoshi is the decision layer for real-estate decarbonisation. It ingests metered data and targets, then converts them into decision-grade outputs: service-performance indicators (SPIs), scenario stress-tests, retrofit economics (NPV/IRR), building comparisons, and IC-ready packs.

Why ROI & risk?

  • Finance speaks NPV — align ops with capital decisions.
  • Markets price exposure — β shows sensitivity to prices/policy/climate.
  • Property ≠ equities — you can’t diversify idiosyncratic risk away.

Pipeline (what Hoshi does)

  1. Ingest: email PDFs/CSVs or connect meters.
  2. Clean & compare: intensity, spend, tCO₂e, coverage.
  3. Signals: NPV / payback; β / sensitivity; a composite for forward ROI.
  4. Scenario-aware: stress-test prices, policy, climate (2030/2050).
  5. Publish: a Building Performance Sheet with lineage.

What makes it different

  • Signals, not scores — built for capital decisions.
  • Alarm → Action → M&V loop with lineage + acceptance criteria.
  • Earth-first frame under a common ecological floor.
